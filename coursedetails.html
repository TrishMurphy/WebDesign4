FAR (First Aid Responder) Course

Course objectives:



At the completion of this course learners will be able to:
  • deal with life threatening or potentially life threatening conditions in the pre-hospital environment until arrival of emergency medical services.
  • provide First Aid Response for non life – threatening but are necessary to prevent further harm before the emergency medical services arrive.
  • provide pre-hospital First Aid Response in a wide range of environments including home and recreational settings.
  • display the requisite personal skills including composure, competence and self-confidence while understanding their limitations.
  • recognise and be able to cope with the additional challenges provided by Special Circumstance for which additional training must be completed
  • deal with life threatening or potentially life threatening conditions in the pre-hospital environment until arrival of emergency medical services.
  • provide First Aid Response for conditions not thought to be life-threatening but are necessary to prevent further harm before the emergency medical services arrive.
  • provide pre-hospital First Aid Response in a wide range of environments including home and recreational settings.
  • display the requisite personal skills including composure, competence and self-confidence while understanding their limitations.

Patient Assessment Incident Procedure

Burns & Electrical Injury Hyopthermia & Hyperthermia

Course Details

One day Basic First Aid

Course objectives



This one day course covers a comprehensive range of topics to prepare participants to administer emergency first aid care.
Participants will learn the up to date protocols in dealing with everyday injuries
and illnesses.


This emergency first aid course will also enable participants to act as a back up to First Aid Responders in the workplace.
Participants will learn a range of practical basic first aid skills,
enabling them to deal competently and confidently with common injuries and illnesses
Included in the training are the basic techniques of cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR),
the use of an Automated External Defibrillator (AED).



Course Content


Child CPR Automated External Defibrillator – AED Recovery (safe airway) position
Airway Obstruction Adult, Child and Infant Impaired Responsiveness Sprain & Strains
Epilepsy Asthma Heart Attack
Stroke Diabetes Allergic


Course Cost €125

Course Details

Cardiac First Responder Course

Course objectives:


To provide personnel involved in Healthcare or frequently exposed to first aid situations with the knowledge and skill to recognise cardiac arrest,
perform Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR) and use an Automated External Defibrillator (AED).

Course Content

Recognise Cardiac Arrest Perform CPR 1 & 2 rescuer
Use an AED Recognise Choking and Treatment of Choking
Assess Stokes (FAST) and Treatment Place patient in Recovery position
Critical Incident Stress Awareness The adminstration of Asprin

Course Cost €75
